## 👁️ Project Overview: Wearable Ambient Light Awareness

### 🌍 Rise of Visual Impairment Globally
Visual impairment affects over **2.2 billion people** worldwide, with at least 1 billion cases preventable or unaddressed [1]. Among these, approximately **36 million individuals are completely blind**, facing life-threatening hazards in ambient light transitions.

### ⌚ Shift to Wearable Assistive Electronics
The healthcare paradigm is moving from hospital-based intervention to continuous, non-invasive, wearable monitoring. For visually impaired patients, real-time ambient light awareness directly:
* 📉 Reduces falls
* 🔄 Supports circadian rhythm regulation
* 🚶‍♂️ Enables safer navigation [2], [3]

### ⚠️ The Wearable Challenge
Commercial assistive devices (e.g., LiDAR glasses, smartphone-based apps, ultrasonic aids) suffer from major drawbacks:
* 🔋 High power budgets (milliwatt-to-watt)
* 🧱 Bulky form factors
* ❌ Fixed detection thresholds that fail across diverse lighting environments [4], [5]

### 🏥 Clinical Scope
This project proposes a **fully integrated CMOS analog comparator operating at 1.8 V** that provides real-time binary ambient light classification (high visibility / low visibility) for wearable skin-patch deployment. 

**Key Technical Achievements:**
* ⚡ **Sub-50 µW** power consumption
* 🎛️ **Adaptive threshold generation** via a resistive divider
* 🔊 **Clean digital output** for direct actuation of haptic or audio feedback
* ✅ Enables reliable, always-on light awareness in ultra-low-power, small-form-factor wearable devices.
